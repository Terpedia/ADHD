Study Methods · Terpedia ADHD Terpene Focus

This methodology defines how Terpedia and human reviewers will design, validate, and document the terpene-focused ADHD study. It aligns tightly with the metastudy, shares data infrastructure, and produces outputs ready for manuscript drafting, regulatory review, and product formulation.

Study Objective

  • Primary question: Do terpene-based interventions improve attention and executive function in ADHD populations?
  • Secondary focus: Which terpene classes (limonene, pinene, linalool, β-caryophyllene) show the greatest signal across delivery formats?
  • Operational goal: Produce a publishable protocol alongside a product-readiness blueprint for Focus on Focus™.

The targeted-study workflow mirrors the metastudy pipeline while narrowing inclusion criteria and highlighting formulation-readiness checkpoints.

Data Sources & Tools

  • Biomedical databases: PubMed, Embase, PsycINFO, Web of Science, Cochrane Library.
  • Trial registries: ClinicalTrials.gov, ISRCTN.
  • Screening platforms: Rayyan, Covidence.
  • Reference management: Zotero, EndNote.
  • Quality instruments: AMSTAR 2, Cochrane RoB 2, CONSORT.
  • Statistical environment: R 4.x with metafor, dplyr, readr.

Eligibility Framework

Population

  • Children, adolescents, or adults diagnosed with ADHD using DSM or ICD criteria.
  • Subgroup data extracted when stratified by age or comorbidity clusters.

Intervention

  • Single-terpene or terpene-dominant formulations (limonene, α-pinene, β-pinene, linalool, β-caryophyllene).
  • Delivery: inhaled aromatherapy, oral capsules, tinctures, or transdermal programs.

Comparators

  • Placebo, standard of care stimulant/non-stimulant therapy, behavioral therapy, or waitlist controls.
  • Active comparators accepted with documented effect direction.

Outcomes

  • Primary: objective attention/executive performance (CPT/TOVA, Go/No-Go, Stroop, N-back, working memory).
  • Secondary: validated rating scales, biomarkers of focus, adverse event profiles.

Study Design

  • Randomized controlled trials or quasi-randomized interventions with explicit terpene dosing.
  • Minimum intervention duration: 1 week. Follow-up extractions when ≥4 weeks available.

Quality review

  • Risk of bias: RoB 2 recorded per domain with consensus adjudication.
  • Reporting quality: CONSORT checklist captured for narrative commentary.
  • Adverse events normalized to MedDRA preferred terms when provided.

Analysis Plan

  • Effect size computation: standardized mean difference (Hedges g) using metafor.
  • Primary model: random-effects (REML) with prediction intervals and heterogeneity (I², τ²).
  • Meta-regressions: compound class, delivery route, treatment duration, age group.
  • Subgroup sensitivity: remove high-risk-of-bias trials; isolate inhaled vs oral routes.
  • Publication bias: funnel plot inspection, Egger regression when ≥10 trials.
  • Supplementary outputs: dose-normalized forest plots, safety event tables, formulation readiness scores.

LLM Prompt Integration

Prompt scaffolds live in ADHD_Terpenes.md. Terpedia uses structured prompts for:

  • Study synopsis (clinical framing, instrumentation, decision gates).
  • Operational plan (screening, randomization, dosing, monitoring).
  • Risk mitigation checklist (safety labs, adverse event tracking, withdrawal criteria).
  • Product translation memo linking trial design to Focus on Focus™ formulation refinements.

Deliverables

Scientific outputs

  • Study protocol manuscript (target: Translational Psychiatry tier journals).
  • Analysis scripts and data dictionary published to GitHub/OSF.
  • Reproducibility appendix with RoB 2 decisions and audit trail.

Product/clinical outputs

  • Formulation brief with dose bands, delivery considerations, stability notes.
  • Clinician quick-start summarizing intervention flow and monitoring.
  • Partner deck linking evidence pipeline to Focus on Focus™ commercialization roadmap.
